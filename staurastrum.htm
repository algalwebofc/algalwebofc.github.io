DESMIDS

Phylum Chlorophyta; sub-phylum Chlorophyceae; order Zygnematales

There are two groups of Desmids: the "Placoderm" and the "Saccoderm" Desmids.

Saccoderm desmids are simple shapes, cylindrical or spindle-shaped, rarely with any median constriction. This group forms the family Mesotaeniaceae in the order Zygnematales, and includes the genera Cylindrocystis, Mesotaenium, Netrium, Roya, Spirotaenia and Tortitaenia.

Placoderm desmids are in the suborder Closteriinae, comprising Closterium, Gonatozygon and Penium, and in the suborder Desmidiinae, Actinotaenium, Cosmarium, Cosmocladium, Euastrum, Micrasterias, Staurastrum, Staurodesmus, and Xanthidium.

There are also filamentous desmids in the Closteriinae, these are not dealt with on these pages, but can be found in the Index: Bambusina, Desmidium, Hyalotheca, Sphaerozosma, Spondylosium.
