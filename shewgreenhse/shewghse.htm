Subaerial algae:

 growing in slime attached to moss on a greenhouse, 2005

Some of these algal species are difficult to identify with certainty,
and these specimens do not correspond exactly with descriptions in
"The Freshwater Algal Flora of the British Isles" (FAFBI).

?Chlorella saccharophila Cells 3-4µm x 6-8µm.  
       
Coccomyxa confluens Cells approx. 3 x 5µm, in pairs within a lamellated sheath  
     
       
       
?Hormotila mucigena filamentous Chlorophyte, cells at the end of or at intervals along the length of branching mucilage tubes; the tubes may be featureless or show layering. It should have a net-like chloroplast, but this feature is not very evident in these examples.  
   
   
       
?Mesotaenium 'Saccoderm' desmid; has a single chloroplast with ridges, sometimes appears stellate in end view; this feature is not mentioned in FAFBI  
The cells are surrounded by a dense structureless mucilage, making it easy to examine them under oil immersion.  
End view of cell: the chloroplast has 4 ridges  
Cell undergoing division  
       
       
       
?Roya These cells are rather shorter than usually described for Roya, a 'saccoderm' desmid. It may be R. obtusa  
  Cell dimensions are approximately 8µm x 30µm.  
?Roya alongside ?Mesotaenium  
       
?Sphaerocystis planctonica Spherical clusters of (?)autospores (approx. 3µm diameter) within a mucilage sheath  
   
  these colonies may be a different species
